PROJECT OVERVIEW

Cornell iGEM’s 2020 project is titled “Lumicure”. Lumicure is a model cancer bacteriotherapy system composed of two parts: genetically modified E. coli and a fluorescent reader device. The E. coli are programmed to release two key components: Trichosanthin, a therapeutic, and mCardinal, a red fluorescent protein. The fluorescent reader device detects the red glow from mCardinal.

Please note that due to COVID-19, we were largely unable to work in the lab this year and so all work on Lumicure was done virtually. Even without a physical product or testing, we hope that we can effectively demonstrate a proof-of-concept in Lumicure for developing an effective cancer treatment in the future.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Lumicure is a bacteriotherapy treatment and tracking system for cancerous malignant tumors. Paired with a patient’s primary therapy, our system aims to identify the physical location of breast cancer metastases and increase therapeutic potency against tumor cells.

Lumicure takes advantage of the fact that cancerous tumors have an immunoprivileged microenvironment and that tumor cells tend to have high lactate concentrations as a result of increased aerobic glycolysis. Even wild-type bacteria tend to colonize these tumors at a significantly higher rate than in healthy tissue. Furthermore, bacteria tend to colonize intracellularly in tumor cells, and therefore would follow malignancies as they detach from primary tumors and to a secondary location.

In order to effectively supplement the patient’s main cancer therapy, the bacteria have been designed to release Trichosanthin, a ribosome-inactivating protein, which targets eukaryotic cells. Our genetically engineered bacteria’s genome also contains a Holin/Antiholin kill switch that allows the bacteria to survive and reproduce in high-lactate tumor environments while inducing apoptosis in low-level lactate concentrations. This ensures that our bacteria are only active in tumor cells and not in healthy human tissue. Additionally, we believe that our E. coli may act as an immunotherapy as well and recruit components of the immune system to the site of the tumor, which are typically suppressed otherwise.

Our bacteria’s genome has been designed to also express mCardinal, a red fluorescent protein. This allows any patient to use our fluorescent reader device, designed by our Product Development subteam, to safely and frequently monitor the tumor growth and metastasis noninvasively through the skin by detection of a red glow. This detection system consists of an excitation filter and emission filter, a dichroic mirror, a laser, and a raspberry pi microcontroller. These elements together allow for vigilant scans at home on a regular basis to help ensure any metastases are caught and treated early.

PROJECT INSPIRATION

Lumicure began with a vision from our passionate team members to design and test a cancer therapeutic system which is a cost-effective alternative to conventional treatment methods, such as chemotherapy or radiation therapy. Specifically, we turned our focus towards breast cancer: this disease is diagnosed in over 270,000 people in the USA every single year. For a given cancer patient in the United States, the average lifetime cancer-related expenses was roughly $50,000 in 2019. With genetically engineered bacteria, however, we hope to lower that number to the order of hundreds of dollars, significantly decreasing the financial burden and making the treatment available to patients of a more diverse socioeconomic background.

In addition to improving the financial outlook for cancer patients, we also intended from the beginning to build a patient-centered monitoring device for the primary tumor and any metastases. This monitoring device, Trichoscan, was spawned due to its perceived ease of use and relatively cheap construction materials. With the tools to monitor the progress of their own cancer in the patients’ hands, we hope to demystify hospital visits and bridge the gap of knowledge between a patient and their doctors.

Throughout the season, we have made it our goal to collaborate with institutions, scientists, doctors, and patients in the Ithaca area to construct Lumicure in the most cost-effective, safe, and patient-friendly way as possible. Though we did not have a chance to build and test our systems, we are nonetheless incredibly proud to present Lumicure to you.
